What are Binaural Beats?

Binaural beats are an auditory illusion perceived by your brain when two slightly different frequency tones are played, one into each ear, typically through **headphones**. Your brain then processes these two distinct tones and creates a "third" perceived tone, which is the mathematical difference between the two input frequencies. This phenomenon is believed to influence brainwave activity, a process called brainwave entrainment. For optimal perception, each base frequency should be between 100-1000 Hz, and the difference between them should be less than 35 Hz.

Different binaural beat frequencies are associated with different brainwave states:

  • Delta (0.5-4 Hz): Deep sleep, relaxation, healing.
  • Theta (4-8 Hz): Deep relaxation, meditation, creativity.
  • Alpha (8-13 Hz): Relaxed alertness, calm, focus.
  • Beta (13-30 Hz): Active thinking, concentration, alertness.
  • Gamma (30-50 Hz): Higher processing, memory, peak performance (upper limit for perceived beat).
